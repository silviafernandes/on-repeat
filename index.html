on repeat

a year in music


As the year ends, I look back and consider which bands and artists I kept on repeat.

Of the 31.968 minutes of music I heard this past year,

these are the albuns I selected.






    1

  • re:member
    • Ólafur Arnalds
    Ólafur Arnalds's re:member cold, somewhat sad self-playing pianos, are probably what the wind in Iceland sounds like.
    "...re:member takes its listeners to a little slice of aural paradise."

    2

  • Parcels
    • Parcels
    Parcels is an up-beat album that proves that Aussies know how to get their funk on.
    "The debut from Parcels is tremendous fun, and it truly doesn’t sound like anything else happening in music today."

    3

  • Matahari
    • L'Imperatrice
    Do I understand the lyrics? Not really. Do I enjoy this disco-infused french début anyway? Absolutely.
    "Matahari is a lunar safari interspersed with organic pleasure and tropical lust."

    4

  • Golden Hour
    • Kacey Musgraves
    Kacey's voice and guitar are smooth enough to make her singing about her daily routine interesting.
    "...the East Texas singer-songwriter is radiant, awestruck, taking the scenic route to the bar just for the hell of it."

    5

  • Wide Awake!
    • Parquet Courts
    Parquet Courts makes me dance to those times I almost started a fight. My life needs more cowbell.
    "An album of joyfully absurd, danceable rock music. It is straightforward but alien, simple but endlessly referential."
